# UI Challenge – Design the Future Official Rulebook

## Theme
Design the Future

## About the Event
UI Challenge is a fast-paced UI/UX design competition that tests participants' creativity, problem-solving skills, and design thinking. Teams are challenged to create an intuitive, visually appealing, and user-centric interface based on a surprise problem statement.

Participants may use any UI/UX design or prototyping tool of their choice (such as Figma, Penpot, Adobe XD, Uizard, Lovable, Galileo AI, Canva, or similar platforms). The focus of the event is not on the software used, but on the quality of the solution, usability, creativity, and overall user experience.

---

## Team Details
- **Team Size:** 2-3 Members
- **Duration:** 60 Minutes

---

## Event Flow (60 Minutes)

| Phase | Duration | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem Statement & Briefing** | 5 Minutes | Participants receive the design challenge, event guidelines, and judging criteria. Queries, if any, are clarified before the competition begins. |
| **Design Sprint** | 45 Minutes | Teams design a user-friendly and visually appealing interface using any UI/UX design or prototyping tool. They are expected to create key screens, define the user flow, and develop a functional prototype. |
| **Presentation & Submission** | 10 Minutes | Teams submit their final prototype and briefly present their solution, explaining their design approach, key features, and user flow. Presentation time may be adjusted depending on the number of participating teams. |

---

## Rules & Regulations
1. Each team must consist of 2-3 participants.
2. Participants must bring their own laptops with the required design software or browser access.
3. Teams may use any UI/UX design or prototyping tool of their choice.
4. AI-assisted design tools are permitted; however, participants must be able to explain their design decisions, workflow, and features during the presentation.
5. The problem statement will be revealed only at the start of the event.
6. All designs must be created during the competition. Pre-designed screens, completed templates, or previously developed project files are strictly prohibited.
7. Internet access may be used only for design platforms, icons, fonts, stock images, and other royalty-free resources, unless otherwise specified by the organizers.
8. Teams must submit their prototype or share the project link before the submission deadline.
9. Late submissions may receive penalty points or be disqualified at the discretion of the organizers.
10. Any form of plagiarism or copying from another team's work will result in immediate disqualification.
11. The decision of the judges and event coordinators will be final and binding.

---

## Judging Criteria

| Criteria | Weightage |
|---|---|
| Creativity & Innovation | 25% |
| User Experience (UX) | 25% |
| Visual Design (UI) | 20% |
| Functionality & User Flow | 20% |
| Presentation & Justification | 10% |

---

## Submission Requirements
Each team must submit:
- Shareable project link or exported prototype (PDF/Image/Prototype Link)
- Team Name
- Team Members' Names
- A brief description of the solution (Maximum 100 words)

---

## What Judges Look For
- Understanding of the problem statement
- Innovative and practical solution
- Simple and intuitive user flow
- Consistent visual design and layout
- Effective use of typography, colors, and spacing
- Overall usability and user experience
- Ability to justify design decisions during the presentation

---

## Tie-Breaker
In case of a tie, preference will be given in the following order:
1. Better User Experience (UX)
2. More Innovative Solution
3. Stronger Presentation & Justification

---

## Winning Tip
> "The best interface is not the most beautiful one—it's the one that solves the user's problem in the simplest and most effective way."
