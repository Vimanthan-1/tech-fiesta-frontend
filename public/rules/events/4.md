# Tech Fiesta – Common Coding Challenge Rule Book

## Event Overview
The Common Coding Challenge is an individual competitive programming event designed to test participants' problem-solving ability, algorithmic thinking, coding skills, accuracy, and speed.

All participants will be given a common competitive programming problem to solve on the CodeChef platform within the allotted time. Participants must independently analyze the problem, develop an efficient solution, and submit their code.

The top-performing participants will be ranked based on the results and evaluation criteria specified by the organizers.

---

## Eligibility
- The event is open to undergraduate and postgraduate students.
- This is an individual event.
- Cross-college participation is permitted.
- Each participant can register only once for the event.

---

## General Rules
1. All participants will be given a common competitive programming problem to solve.
2. The problem will be provided through the CodeChef platform.
3. Participants must solve the problem individually within the allotted time.
4. Participants may use only the programming languages permitted for the contest on the CodeChef platform.
5. Participants are not allowed to communicate with or seek assistance from other participants during the event.
6. The use of AI tools, mobile phones, external websites, personal notes, pre-written code, or any other unauthorized external assistance is strictly prohibited.
7. Participants must submit their solutions within the specified time limit.
8. Submissions made after the contest ends will not be considered for evaluation.
9. Participants are responsible for ensuring that their code is submitted successfully before the contest ends.
10. Any form of cheating, plagiarism, code sharing, or unfair means will result in immediate disqualification.
11. Participants must report to the venue at least 30 minutes before the scheduled start of the event.
12. The decision of the judges and organizing committee will be final and binding.

---

## Event Flow
- Participant Registration
- Participant Verification
- Rules Briefing
- CodeChef Contest Access
- Common Problem Reveal
- Coding Challenge Begins
- Solution Submission
- Submission Evaluation
- Result Compilation
- Winner Announcement

---

## Contest Format
All participants will receive the same competitive programming problem at the beginning of the event.

Participants must:
- Analyze the given problem independently.
- Develop and implement an appropriate algorithm.
- Write and debug their solution within the allotted time.
- Submit their solution through the designated CodeChef contest.
- Ensure that their final submission is successfully recorded before the contest ends.

Participants may make multiple submissions within the contest duration, subject to the rules and restrictions set by the organizers.

---

## Evaluation and Ranking Criteria
Participants will primarily be evaluated based on the correctness of their submitted solution.

The following factors may be considered for determining the final ranking:
- Successful acceptance of the solution.
- Number of test cases or subtasks passed, where applicable.
- Time taken to achieve a successful submission.
- Penalties for incorrect submissions, if applicable under the configured contest format.

The final ranking will be determined based on the official contest standings and evaluation method announced by the organizers.

---

## Tie-Breaker Criteria
In case of a tie between two or more participants, the tie will be resolved based on the following criteria, as applicable:
1. Participant who achieved the successful submission earlier.
2. Participant with fewer incorrect submissions before the successful submission.
3. Performance based on the official CodeChef contest standings.
4. If the tie still remains, the final decision will be made by the judges and organizing committee.

---

## Code of Conduct
- Maintain professional behavior throughout the event.
- Respect the judges, organizers, volunteers, and fellow participants.
- Participants must follow all instructions provided by the event coordinators.
- Communication or collaboration with other participants during the contest is strictly prohibited.
- The use of AI tools or unauthorized external assistance during the event is strictly prohibited.
- Copying or sharing code with another participant will result in immediate disqualification.
- Any attempt to interfere with another participant's system, account, or submission will result in immediate disqualification.
- Any form of misconduct, cheating, or unethical behavior may result in immediate disqualification.

---

## Prizes and Rewards
- **Top 3 Participants:** CodeChef Pro subscription.
- **Top 20 Participants:** Coupon rewards.

Prize and coupon distribution will be subject to the terms and conditions specified by the organizers and CodeChef, where applicable.

---

## Organizer's Decision
The organizing committee reserves the right to modify the event format, problem difficulty, contest duration, evaluation criteria, prize structure, or schedule if required.

Any significant changes will be communicated to all participants before the commencement of the event.

The decision of the judges and organizing committee will be final and binding.
