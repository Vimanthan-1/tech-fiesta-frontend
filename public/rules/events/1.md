# Tech Fiesta – Paper Presentation Rule Book

## Event Overview
The Paper Presentation event provides a platform for students to present innovative ideas, research work, and solutions to real-world problems. The event follows the theme of **Open Innovation**, encouraging participants to think creatively and present original ideas across any domain.

---

## Eligibility
- The event is open to undergraduate and postgraduate students.
- This is an individual event.
- Cross-college participation is permitted.

---

## General Rules
1. The presentation topic must align with the theme of Open Innovation.
2. The work presented must be the participant's original idea. Any form of plagiarism will result in immediate disqualification.
3. Participants must bring their presentation (PPT) on a pen drive and are advised to keep a backup copy.
4. The presentation should be prepared using a PPT.
5. Each participant will be allotted:
   - **Presentation:** 10 minutes
   - **Judge Q&A:** 5 minutes
6. Exceeding the allotted time may result in a deduction of marks.
7. Participants must report to the venue at least 30 minutes before their scheduled presentation.
8. The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

---

## Event Flow
- Participant Registration
- Paper Presentation
- Judge Q&A
- AI Evaluation Round
- Result Compilation
- Winner Announcement

---

## AI Evaluation Round
Following each presentation, an AI-generated solution for the same problem statement will be displayed. 
The participant must:
- Compare their proposed solution with the AI-generated solution.
- Explain why their approach is more effective, practical, innovative, or suitable for solving the problem.
- Convince the judges by highlighting the strengths and uniqueness of their own solution.

The AI-generated evaluation will consider the following parameters:

| Criteria | Score |
| :--- | :--- |
| Innovation | /10 |
| Feasibility | /10 |
| Social Impact | /10 |
| Scalability | /10 |

This round evaluates the participant's critical thinking, technical understanding, and ability to justify their design decisions.

---

## Judging Criteria

| Criteria | Marks |
| :--- | :--- |
| Problem Identification | 5 |
| Research Depth | 5 |
| Technical Accuracy | 5 |
| Innovation in Concept | 5 |
| **Total** | **20** |

The judges may also consider:
- Clarity of explanation
- Communication skills
- Confidence
- Practical applicability
- Ability to answer technical questions

---

## Code of Conduct
- Maintain professional behavior throughout the event.
- Respect the judges, organizers, volunteers, and fellow participants.
- Any form of plagiarism, misconduct, or unethical behavior will lead to immediate disqualification.
- The use of AI tools or external assistance during the live presentation or Q&A is not permitted unless explicitly approved by the organizers.

---

## Awards
- Winner
- First Runner-Up
- Second Runner-Up

## Organizer's Decision
The organizing committee reserves the right to modify the event schedule or rules if required. Any changes will be communicated to all participants before the commencement of the event. The decision of the judges will be final.
