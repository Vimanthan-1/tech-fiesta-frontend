# Tech Fiesta – Chess Championship Rule Book

## Event Overview
The Chess Championship is a competitive chess tournament designed to test participants' strategy, tactical skills, foresight, and concentration. The event transitions from a digital format in the initial stages to a classic physical game in the finals.

---

## Eligibility
- The event is open to undergraduate and postgraduate students.
- This is an individual participation event.
- Cross-college participation is permitted.

---

## Event Format
The competition will be conducted in two rounds:
- **Round 1 – Online Qualifier**
- **Final Round – Offline (Physical Chess Board)**

---

## Round 1: Online Qualifier
- Participants will play using their mobile phones.
- The matches will be conducted via an online chess platform (details and links will be shared prior to the event).
- Each participant must:
  - Ensure a stable internet connection.
  - Join the match on time.
- Matches will follow a knockout or points-based system (to be specified by the organizers).
- Winners of Round 1 will qualify for the final round.

---

## Final Round: Offline Match
- Qualified participants will play using a physical chess board.
- Matches will be conducted face-to-face under supervision.
- Standard chess rules will be followed.
- The winner of this round will be declared the Champion.

---

## General Rules
1. Fair play is mandatory — no external assistance, engine assistance, or cheating of any form is allowed.
2. Participants must follow the instructions of the event coordinators.
3. Any misconduct, stalling, or violation of rules will lead to immediate disqualification.
4. The organizer’s decision will be final and binding.

---

## Additional Notes
- Reporting time and match schedules will be shared in advance.
- Participants should be ready with their devices for Round 1 and must be on time for the finals.
